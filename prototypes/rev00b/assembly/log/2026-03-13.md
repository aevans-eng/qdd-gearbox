# 2026-03-13 — Rev 00A Testing Review & Rev 00B Scoping

---

### — Rev 00A Test Tracker Update

**What:** Reviewed all four tests (T-001 through T-004) against Rev 00A prototype findings. Updated test tracker with decisions, observations, and design direction for Rev 00B.

**Decisions:**

- **T-001 (Gear mesh root cause):** Postponed to Rev 00B. Output shaft is clearly undersized — needs enlarging with a larger top bearing. Not worth modifying current part. Rev 00B will thread directly into plastic instead of using heat inserts (simpler, reprint is cheap).
- **T-002 (Lid drag):** Root cause identified without formal testing. The lid is double-constraining — it clamps the ring gear to the housing AND constrains the carrier top. Tolerance stackup through the carrier assembly (body shaft → planets → shoulder heights → bearing press depths) pushes the carrier higher than nominal, so the lid presses on it. Fix for Rev 00B: decouple ring gear clamping from carrier constraint. Options include adding axial clearance, deepening the lid bearing recess, or spacing the lid out.
- **T-003 (Shoulder deformation):** Visual inspection post-run. Bottom carrier shoulders are intact. Top carrier has print quality issue — printed output shaft up gives good top bearing surface but bad planet bearing shoulders (artifacts from overhangs). Three redesign options identified: (1) better supports, (2) metal spacers on inner bearing race with flat top carrier and taller bottom carrier walls, (3) geometry change so bearing shoulders are flush with gear cutouts and sit directly on build plate.
- **T-004 (Carrier indexing):** About to run — witness marks + video with revision label written on parts.

**Rev 00B changes scoped so far:**
- Larger output shaft + larger top bearing
- Threaded into plastic (no heat inserts)
- Lid constraint redesign (decouple ring gear + carrier)
- Top carrier print quality fix (TBD which approach)

# 2026-02-14 — QDD Gearbox Work Log

### 14:XX — Design Decision: Interior-Out Dimensioning

**What:** Decided to constrain interior dimensions first (internal spacings, clearances, gear heights) and let gearbox height be a dependent variable.

**Why:** Internal dimensions are most critical for reliable function — bearings need proper seating, planets need clearance to spin free. Packaging constraints (exterior dimensions) are secondary. Better to get the internals right and let the envelope be what it needs to be, rather than force internals into a fixed box.

**Design context:** The carrier-planet interface requires:
- Radial constraint: bearings locating planets on pins
- Axial constraint: shoulders/spacers/snap rings to hold planets vertically
- Clamshell carrier design: fastener paths, bearing pockets, spacers for clearance

**Sketch:** Cross-section showing carrier clamshell (red/magenta) with planet gears (blue) on bearings, and exploded view of axial stack-up.

**Note:** Fasteners will put axial loads on the standard ball bearings — not ideal, but acceptable for MVP prototype using existing parts.

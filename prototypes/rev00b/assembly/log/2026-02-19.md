# 2026-02-19 — QDD Gearbox Work Log

### — Engineering Decision: Validate FDM Tolerances with Calibration Coupon

**What:** Before printing real gearbox parts, printing a calibration coupon to test-fit actual bearings, bolts, and inserts into features modeled at our planned FDM target dimensions. Direct hardware validation instead of caliper-only measurement.

**Why:** Theoretical offsets need real-world validation. Testing with actual hardware answers "does this fit work?" directly — catches things calipers can't (ovality, layer texture, surface grip). If a fit is wrong, then measure with calipers to quantify the adjustment.

**Details:** Full coupon design (layout, cross-sections, CATIA steps, fit-test procedure) in [`10-tolerance-scheme.md` §6](10-tolerance-scheme.md). Features use actual FDM target dims from the tolerance scheme, with shoulder geometry for bearing seating.

### — Calibration Coupon Results

**What:** First coupon printed and tested with real hardware.

**Wins:**
- Main bearing (6805-2RS) → finger press fit at ⌀37.10 bore. Perfect transition fit. Keep offset.
- Post (⌀25.10) printed 25.01mm — shaft shrink as expected.

**Needs adjustment:**
- Planet bore (⌀14.00) printed 13.75mm — 685ZZ won't go in. Need +0.25mm offset.
- Small holes shrink proportionally more: ⌀5→4.65, ⌀3.4→~3.0. Need ~+0.35-0.40mm on small features.
- M3 heat-set inserts are bigger than assumed: 6.05mm OD w/ ribs, 4.96mm body. Pilot hole needs resizing from 4.2mm → ~5.3mm modeled.

**Key insight:** Smaller features shrink proportionally more on this printer. ⌀37 lost ~0.3%, ⌀3.4 lost ~12%.

**Measurement uncertainty:** Small hole measurements (⌀3–5mm) are approximate — calipers are imprecise on small bores. Bearing fits (⌀37, ⌀25, ⌀14) are reliable (hardware fit-testing = direct pass/fail). Also: bearing shaft post measured 25.01mm but bearing was **not test-fitted onto it** — dimension looks right but fit feel not confirmed.

**Hypothesis:** Z-seam may contribute to small hole tightness — random seam placement means seam bumps land inside bores. On small diameters, seam bump is a larger % of circumference. Worth testing aligned seam (away from bores) on coupon v2.

**Details:** Full measurement table and updated offset recommendations in [`10-tolerance-scheme.md` §6 — Calibration Results](10-tolerance-scheme.md).

## Locked Gear Configuration Explained

A planetary gear has 4 elements: Sun (S), Planet (P), Ring (R), and Carrier (C).
By locking one element and choosing input/output, you get different ratios:

| Locked | Input | Output | Ratio Formula | Example (S=6,R=24) | Type |
|--------|-------|--------|---------------|-------------------|------|
| **RING** | **Sun** | **Carrier** | **(R+S)/S = 1+R/S** | **5.00:1** | **Reduction (THIS SWEEP)** |
| Ring | Carrier | Sun | S/(R+S) | 0.20:1 | Speed increase |
| Sun | Carrier | Ring | R/(R+S) | 0.80:1 | Speed increase |
| Sun | Ring | Carrier | (R+S)/R | 1.25:1 | Mild reduction |
| Carrier | Sun | Ring | -R/S | -4.00:1 | **Reverses direction** |
| Carrier | Ring | Sun | -S/R | -0.25:1 | **Reverses direction** |

**This sweep uses: RING LOCKED, SUN INPUT, CARRIER OUTPUT**
- The RING gear is held stationary (bolted to housing/frame)
- The SUN gear is the input (connected to motor shaft)
- The CARRIER is the output (connected to load)
- This gives the highest reduction ratio without direction reversal

## Planet Overlap Design Rules

### The Problem
In v1, the S=4 config with N=4 planets had overlapping planet gears. The planets physically intersected each other.

### Overlap Geometry Analysis
For N planets evenly spaced on a carrier of radius 1 (normalized):
```
Planet center spacing = 2 * sin(pi/N)
Planet outer diameter = (P + tooth_addendum) / (S + P)

For no overlap: spacing > diameter * safety_margin
                2*sin(pi/N) > (P + 1.5)/(S + P) * 1.1
```

### Validation Results from v1 Configs

| S | P | R | N | Spacing | Diameter | Clearance | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---------|----------|-----------|--------|
| 4 | 6 | 16 | 4 | 1.414 | 1.512 | **-16.5%** | **OVERLAP** |
| 4 | 6 | 16 | 3 | 1.732 | 1.512 | 4.6% | OK |
| 5 | 8 | 21 | 3 | 1.732 | 1.471 | 7.8% | OK |
| 6 | 9 | 24 | 3 | 1.732 | 1.408 | 13.0% | OK |
| 7 | 10 | 27 | 3 | 1.732 | 1.360 | 17.4% | OK |

### Design Rule Summary
For ~5:1 ratio configurations:
- **Maximum N = 3 planets** (for adequate clearance)
- Larger sun gears (more teeth) give more clearance
- S=4 is borderline (only 4.6% clearance with N=3)
- S=7 has best clearance (17.4%)

## Key Design Insights

1. **More sun teeth = more clearance** - S=7 has 17.4% vs S=4 has only 4.6%
2. **N=3 is the maximum for ~5:1 ratio** - N=4 causes overlap for all configs
3. **Even sun teeth give exact 5:1** - S=4, S=6 are exact; S=5, S=7 require compromise
4. **S=6 is the sweet spot** - Exact 5:1 ratio with good 13% clearance
5. **Ratio tolerance +/-1** - All configs within 4:1 to 6:1 range

## Recommendations for Next Iteration

- If clearance is critical: Use S=7, P=10, R=27 (17.4% clearance, ratio 4.86:1)
- If exact 5:1 needed: Use S=6, P=9, R=24 (13% clearance)
- If compact size needed: Use S=4, P=6, R=16 (tight 4.6% clearance)
- Consider trying different b values to affect tooth profile and clearance
- Could explore N=2 for even more clearance (but less load sharing)
